# Sprint 63 Customer Success Account Health Adoption Playbooks Roadmap

## Purpose

This document defines Sprint 63 for Customer Success, Account Health, and Adoption Playbooks foundation.

Sprint 63 should create a reusable customer success layer for account health summaries, adoption health signals, churn/risk placeholders, success playbooks, playbook steps/tasks, customer interventions, renewal/support links, product analytics/feedback/help links, and audit history.

## Sprint Goal

Enable platform and customer success teams to understand organization health, identify adoption risks, run success playbooks, coordinate interventions, and connect support, renewal, and product signals safely.

## Why This Sprint

The platform now has signals that can support proactive customer success:

- product analytics and adoption telemetry
- experimentation and controlled rollouts
- feedback and feature requests
- support operations
- subscription billing and revenue operations
- organization lifecycle and onboarding
- knowledge base and help center
- notification and workflow foundations

Without a shared customer success foundation, account health may be managed manually across spreadsheets, support notes, billing data, and informal conversations.

## Work Package 1: Account Health Summary Metadata

Prepare account health records with:

- organization
- health status placeholder
- adoption status placeholder
- support status placeholder
- billing/renewal status placeholder
- last reviewed date placeholder
- owner safe label

## Work Package 2: Adoption Health Signals

Prepare adoption signal placeholders for:

- onboarding completion
- active module usage
- key workflow completion
- feature adoption trend
- knowledge base engagement
- product communication acknowledgement placeholder

## Work Package 3: Risk and Churn Signal Placeholder

Prepare risk signal placeholders for:

- low adoption
- high support volume
- payment/renewal warning
- negative feedback trend
- inactive users
- failed onboarding milestone

Signals are reviewable indicators, not automatic churn decisions.

## Work Package 4: Success Playbook Metadata

Prepare playbook records for:

- onboarding playbook
- adoption recovery playbook
- renewal readiness playbook
- support escalation playbook
- new feature adoption playbook
- branch/network expansion playbook

## Work Package 5: Playbook Steps and Tasks

Prepare step/task placeholders with:

- task title
- owner safe label
- due date placeholder
- status
- related signal placeholder
- escalation placeholder

## Work Package 6: Customer Intervention Placeholder

Prepare intervention/action placeholders for:

- schedule check-in
- send help article
- trigger support follow-up
- recommend training/policy acknowledgement
- notify account owner
- review billing/renewal readiness

## Work Package 7: Renewal Support Product Linkage

Prepare links to:

- subscription billing status
- support cases
- product analytics summaries
- feedback/feature requests
- knowledge base articles
- product announcements

## Work Package 8: Customer Success Audit History

Prepare audit history for health review, playbook changes, intervention actions, and risk signal reviews.

## Work Package 9: Flutter Foundation

Prepare screens or placeholders for:

- customer success dashboard
- account health list
- account health detail
- adoption signal summary
- risk signal summary
- success playbook list
- playbook task board
- intervention/action list
- renewal/support linkage view
- customer success audit history

## Out of Scope

Do not implement:

- automated churn prediction as final decision
- intrusive customer surveillance
- unrestricted cross-tenant account health visibility
- automatic billing changes or renewal commitments
- replacement of Support Operations
- replacement of Subscription Billing
- AI-only customer success decisions

## Completion Standard

Sprint 63 is complete when account health summaries, adoption signals, risk signals, success playbooks, playbook tasks, interventions, renewal/support/product links, and customer success audit history are available through permission-protected and auditable foundations.

## Final Rule

Customer success should turn platform signals into proactive support actions while preserving tenant privacy, human review, and system boundaries.
