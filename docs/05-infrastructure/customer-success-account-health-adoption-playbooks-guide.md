# Customer Success Account Health Adoption Playbooks Guide

## Purpose

This document defines operating guidance for account health summaries, adoption health signals, risk/churn signals, success playbooks, playbook tasks, interventions, renewal/support/product links, success notes, and customer success audit history.

The goal is to help teams proactively support organizations without violating privacy or turning signals into automatic decisions.

## Principle

Customer success should convert platform signals into reviewed support actions.

Account health signals are indicators, not final churn predictions or billing decisions.

## Data Sources

Customer success operations may use:

- account health summary metadata
- adoption health signal placeholder
- risk/churn signal placeholder
- success playbook metadata
- playbook step/task placeholder
- customer intervention/action placeholder
- renewal/subscription/support/product link
- success note placeholder
- audit records

## Account Health Direction

Account health records should capture:

```text
organization_id
health_status_placeholder
adoption_status_placeholder
support_status_placeholder
billing_renewal_status_placeholder
last_reviewed_date_placeholder
owner_safe_label
```

## Adoption Signal Direction

Adoption signals may include:

- onboarding completion
- active module usage
- key workflow completion
- feature adoption trend
- knowledge base engagement
- product communication acknowledgement placeholder

## Risk Signal Direction

Risk signals may include:

- low adoption
- high support volume
- payment/renewal warning
- negative feedback trend
- inactive users
- failed onboarding milestone

Risk signals should be reviewed before any customer-facing decision.

## Playbook Direction

Success playbooks may include:

- onboarding playbook
- adoption recovery playbook
- renewal readiness playbook
- support escalation playbook
- new feature adoption playbook
- branch/network expansion playbook

## Playbook Task Direction

Playbook tasks should capture:

- task title
- owner safe label
- due date placeholder
- status
- related signal placeholder
- escalation placeholder

## Intervention Direction

Interventions may include:

- schedule check-in
- send help article
- trigger support follow-up
- recommend training/policy acknowledgement
- notify account owner
- review billing/renewal readiness

## Renewal Support Product Link Direction

Customer success links may connect to:

- subscription billing status
- support cases
- product analytics summaries
- feedback/feature requests
- knowledge base articles
- product announcements

## Notes Direction

Success notes should be safe, permission-controlled, and free of unnecessary sensitive data.

## Audit Direction

Audit should record:

- account health summary created or updated
- adoption signal reviewed placeholder
- risk signal reviewed placeholder
- success playbook created or updated
- playbook task created or completed placeholder
- intervention action created or completed placeholder
- renewal/support/product link changed
- success note added placeholder

## Data Quality Warnings

Warn or flag when:

- account health has not been reviewed recently
- low adoption has no active playbook
- high support volume has no intervention
- renewal warning has no account owner
- onboarding is incomplete after expected period placeholder
- linked support case is unresolved for long period placeholder
- success note contains sensitive details placeholder

## Final Rule

Customer success operations must preserve tenant privacy, avoid automatic churn or billing decisions, and keep proactive support actions traceable.
